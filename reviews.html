In-depth looks at films worth your time.

Your Name (Kimi no Na wa)

Director: Makoto Shinkai | 2016 | Anime

Makoto Shinkai's Your Name is a film that earns its emotional payoff. On the surface, it is a body-swap story between two teenagers, but Shinkai uses this premise to explore themes of connection, fate, and the way we remember people who shaped us.

The animation is stunning, with some of the most beautiful depictions of light and sky ever put to screen. What sets this film apart is how it balances its fantasy elements with genuine emotional stakes. By the final act, you are fully invested in whether these two characters will find each other.

Rating: 9/10

Parasite

Director: Bong Joon-ho | 2019 | Thriller/Drama

Parasite defies genre. It starts as a dark comedy about class, shifts into a tense thriller, and ends as something else entirely. Bong Joon-ho controls tone with precision, never letting the audience settle into comfort.

The film works because it refuses to simplify its characters. Neither family is purely good or evil. Both are products of a system that pits them against each other. The house itself becomes a character, its architecture reflecting the social stratification the film critiques.

Rating: 10/10

Arrival

Director: Denis Villeneuve | 2016 | Science Fiction

Arrival is science fiction for people who want to think. It uses an alien encounter not for spectacle but as a vehicle to explore language, time, and grief. Amy Adams delivers one of the most understated and powerful performances of the decade.

The film rewards rewatching. Details that seem insignificant on first viewing become heartbreaking once you understand the full picture. This is a film about how we find meaning in impermanence.

Rating: 9/10
